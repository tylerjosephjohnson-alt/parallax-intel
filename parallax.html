About Vantage

Vantage is an automated geopolitical intelligence product. Stories are generated without human writers. This page explains the editorial standards we hold ourselves to, so you can judge our work against them.

Seven principles

1No sides. What is.

Vantage reports what happened, what is known, and what follows. We do not characterize actors as heroic, villainous, justified, or illegitimate. If you can tell which side Vantage is on from reading a story, we have failed. This applies symmetrically — every nationality, ethnicity, religious group, political faction, and state actor is written about in the same tone using the same standards.

2Sourced or silent.

Every factual claim comes from a named source or is explicitly labeled as analysis. We do not say "experts believe," "reports suggest," or "it is understood that." If we cannot attribute a claim, we do not publish it. When sources disagree, we say so and name which source says what.

3Numbers are numbers.

When actors disagree on a quantifiable claim — casualties, damage, economic figures, military losses — we report all stated figures with their sources. We do not use comparative qualifiers like "higher," "lower," "much," "far," or "dramatically" to characterize one side's figure relative to another's.

4Uncertainty is a feature, not a flaw.

When we don't know something, we say we don't know. When a claim is contested, we say it is contested. When confidence is low, we label it low. Calibrated confidence is itself a product — when Vantage says "moderate confidence," we commit to a track record against which that label can be measured.

5Base rates over narratives.

Predictions and analytical claims are grounded in historical comparables with explicit sample sizes. "This has happened 4 of 5 times before in similar circumstances" is the kind of claim we make. "Analysts fear X" is not. Where base rates don't exist, we say "insufficient comparables" and decline to assign probability.

6Plain language.

We write for an intelligent reader who is not a specialist in the specific region or topic. Acronyms are expanded on first mention. Historical references are contextualized. Technical terms explain themselves.

7One event, every perspective.

Every story explicitly surfaces how the event is being reported by at least three named sources, including ideologically or geographically distinct ones. We do not present a single framing as the framing.

How we handle mistakes

Different kinds of errors need different responses. Vantage uses four tiers: Update (new information appended above original), Correction (specific error struck through and corrected in place), Retraction (story pulled, URL serves retraction notice), and Systemic Issue Notice (pattern-level failure acknowledged publicly). We never make silent edits.

Accuracy track record

Predictions and confidence-labeled claims are logged and scored against a published accuracy page. When we say "moderate confidence" across 100 predictions, what percentage were right? That number is public. If the track record diverges from the label's intended meaning, we recalibrate and say so openly.
